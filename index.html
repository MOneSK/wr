What makes a Band 5–6 paragraph fail
❌ Band 5–6 Example — Topic: Should governments invest in renewable energy?
There are many benefits of renewable energy. [VAGUE TOPIC]
It is good for the environment. [NO REASON]
Many countries use solar power now. [WEAK EXAMPLE]
This shows that renewable energy is important. [REPEATS TOPIC]
Governments should invest in it. [NO LOGIC]
Problems: Vague topic sentence · No reason WHY · No HOW mechanism · Unnamed example · Conclusion repeats the opener.

Introduction & Conclusion
A Band 7+ introduction is exactly two sentences. A conclusion is two to three. This section teaches you the template, shows what good looks like, and drills you on both.
① Introduction
② Conclusion
③ Practice
The rule
Two sentences. That's it.
Sentence 1 (Background): Paraphrase the question in your own words. Do not copy the prompt.
Sentence 2 (Thesis): State your position clearly. Commit to a stance.

Strong vs Weak — study these carefully
❌ Band 5 Introduction
Nowadays social media is very popular all over the world. Many people use it every day. There are many advantages and disadvantages of social media. Some people think it is good and some think it is bad. In this essay I will discuss both sides.
Five vague sentences · No real thesis · "I will discuss both sides" is not a position
✓ Band 7–8 Introduction
As digital technology becomes increasingly embedded in everyday life, the question of whether social media causes more harm than good to young people has generated significant public debate. I strongly believe that, while these platforms offer certain social benefits, their overall impact on young users' mental health is predominantly negative, primarily due to the culture of comparison they actively encourage.
Paraphrases the question · Commits to a clear position · Gives a specific reason in the thesis
Do / Don't checklist
✓ DO
Paraphrase the question in your own words
State your position clearly in the thesis
Keep to 2 sentences (40–60 words total)
✗ DON'T
Copy words directly from the question
Write "In this essay I will..."
Include evidence or reasons in the intro
The rule
2–3 sentences. Never introduce new ideas.
Sentence 1: Signal the end + restate your thesis in new words.
Sentence 2: Summarise your two main points briefly.
Sentence 3 (optional): A final broader observation or recommendation.
Conclusion templates — click to copy
Strong vs Weak
❌ Band 5 Conclusion
In conclusion, there are many advantages and disadvantages. People should think carefully about this issue. I hope society finds a good solution in the future.
No position restated · No key points summarised · "I hope" is meaningless filler
✓ Band 7–8 Conclusion
In conclusion, I firmly maintain that social media is more harmful than beneficial to young people's mental health, primarily because it promotes unhealthy comparison and reduces face-to-face social interaction. While platforms can be valuable communication tools, these psychological harms outweigh any social benefits, suggesting that stricter regulation of social media use among minors is urgently needed.
Restates thesis · Summarises both main points · Ends with a broader recommendation
